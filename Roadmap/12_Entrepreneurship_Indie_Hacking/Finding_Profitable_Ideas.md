# **Phase 12: Entrepreneurship - Finding Profitable Ideas**

A great product starts with a great problem. If you build something nobody wants, no amount of marketing will save you.

---

## **1. Look for "High Pain" Problems**
People pay to solve problems that:
- **Save them time:** (e.g., An automated report generator).
- **Make them money:** (e.g., A tool to find high-intent leads).
- **Help them stay legal:** (e.g., A GDPR compliance checker).

---

## **2. The "Niche" Strategy**
Don't build "Gmail for Everyone." Build "Gmail for Dentists."
- By narrowing your focus, you make it easier to find your customers and talk to them in their language.

---

## **3. Where to Hunt for Ideas?**
- **Reddit:** Look for people complaining in subreddits like `r/smallbusiness` or `r/realestate`. Search for keywords like "How do I," "I hate it when," or "Is there a tool for."
- **G2/Capterra:** Look at 1-star and 2-star reviews of popular software. What are people missing? What is too complicated?
- **Twitter/X:** Follow the #IndieHackers community.

---

## **4. Validating Without Code**
Before you write a single line of code, try to "sell" the idea.
- Create a simple Landing Page (using Carrd or Framer).
- Add a "Join Waitlist" or "Pre-order" button.
- Run ₹500 worth of ads. If people click and sign up, you have a winner.

---
