# **Phase 12: Entrepreneurship - Marketing for Developers**

As a developer, your instinct is to hide and code for 6 months. **Don't do that.** If nobody knows your product exists, nobody will buy it. Marketing is just "telling people how you can help them."

---

## **1. Building in Public**
The most effective marketing for solo founders.
- **Where:** Twitter/X, LinkedIn, or a personal blog.
- **What to share:**
    - A screenshot of a new feature you just built.
    - A struggle you had with a bug (and how you fixed it).
    - Your first user signup (milestone).
    - Your current MRR (transparency).
- **Why:** It builds trust and an audience before you even launch.

---

## **2. Content Marketing (SEO Basics)**
Write articles that answer the questions your target users are searching for.
- *Example:* If you build a "PDF to Excel" tool, write a blog post: *"5 Ways to Convert PDF to Excel for Free (2026 Guide)."*
- **SEO Tip:** Use simple tools like **Google Trends** or **Ubersuggest** to see what keywords have high volume.

---

## **3. Where to Launch?**
1. **Product Hunt:** The Super Bowl of startups. Great for a big initial spike.
2. **Hacker News (Show HN):** Brutally honest feedback. 
3. **Reddit:** Find the specific subreddit for your niche. **Don't spam.** Be helpful first, then mention your tool.
4. **Indie Hackers:** A community of people doing exactly what you are doing.

---

## **4. Cold Outreach**
If you have a B2B (Business to Business) tool, find 50 potential customers on LinkedIn and send them a **non-salesy** message:
*"Hi [Name], I noticed you do X. I built a small tool that automates [Pain Point]. Would love to know if this would be useful for you? No pressure!"*

---

## **🎯 Exercise for You:**
Create a Twitter/X account (if you don't have one) and post your first #BuildInPublic tweet: *"Today I'm starting my journey to $1k MRR by building a [Brief Idea]. Follow along for the ups and downs!"*
