# **Phase 13: AI Infrastructure - Hardware & Clusters**

To own the model, you must eventually own the silicon. Your hardware strategy will evolve as your startup grows.

---

## **1. The Developer Machine (Year 0-1)**
- **Your Current Laptop:** Great for writing code, testing logic, and simple data cleaning.
- **The Workaround:** Use **Google Colab (Free/Pro)** or **Kaggle Kernels** to get free access to Tesla T4 or A100 GPUs.

---

## **2. The Solo-Founder Workstation (Year 1-2)**
When you have your first $500/month in MRR, invest in a local machine.
- **GPU:** NVIDIA RTX 3090 or 4090 (24GB VRAM is the minimum for serious fine-tuning).
- **RAM:** 64GB+.
- **Storage:** NVMe SSDs for fast data loading.

---

## **3. The Cloud Cluster (Scaling Phase)**
When you need to train giant models or serve thousands of users.
- **Lambda Labs / RunPod:** Much cheaper than AWS/Google Cloud for GPU rentals.
- **H100 Clusters:** The "Gold Standard" for training. Rent these by the hour.

---

## **4. The Long-Term Vision (Year 10+)**
Building your own **In-House GPU Cluster**.
- Owning your own racks of A100/H100 servers.
- Running your own "Inference Engine" (vLLM, TGI) to serve your GaaS products with zero latency and zero API costs.

---

## **5. Managing Energy & Cooling**
As you scale, the cost of electricity and cooling becomes a major business factor. Successful AI founders often locate their hardware in regions with cheap, green energy.

---

## **🎯 Exercise for You:**
Research the price of a used **NVIDIA RTX 3090** on the market today. This should be your first major "Startup Investment" once you have revenue.
