# **Phase 15: Quantum - Physics Basics**

To program a quantum computer, you don't need to be a physicist, but you must understand three core concepts.

---

## **1. Superposition**
A qubit can exist in a state of 0 and 1 at the same time. Only when you **measure** it does it "collapse" into a definite 0 or 1.
- *Think of it like:* Every possibility exists until you make a choice.

---

## **2. Entanglement**
When two qubits become "entangled," they are linked across any distance. If you measure one, the other instantly changes its state.
- Einstein called this **"Spooky action at a distance."**
- In computing, this allows us to create massive amounts of "Parallelism" that classical bits can never match.

---

## **3. Interference**
Just like waves in the ocean, quantum states can add up (Constructive) or cancel each other out (Destructive).
- **Quantum Algorithms** work by canceling out the wrong answers and magnifying the correct ones.

---

## **4. The Bloch Sphere**
The visualization of a single qubit. Instead of a 0/1 switch, it is a **Sphere**. Any point on the surface of the sphere is a valid quantum state.

---
