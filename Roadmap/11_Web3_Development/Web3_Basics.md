# **Phase 11: Web3 Development - Basics**

Web3 is the next evolution of the internet, where users own their data and transactions are verified by a decentralized network instead of a central company.

---

## **1. What is Blockchain?**
A blockchain is a **Distributed Ledger**. Imagine a Google Sheet that everyone can see, but nobody can delete or change an old entry. You can only add new rows at the bottom.

- **Decentralization:** No single entity (like a bank or Zoho) controls the network.
- **Immutability:** Once data is written, it cannot be changed.
- **Transparency:** Anyone can verify any transaction.

---

## **2. Wallets (MetaMask)**
In Web3, your "Account" is a Wallet. 
- **Private Key:** Your secret password. **NEVER share this.**
- **Public Address:** Like your username or email. People use this to send you funds.
- **MetaMask:** The most popular browser extension wallet for interacting with Web3 apps.

---

## **3. Smart Contracts**
Smart contracts are pieces of code stored on the blockchain. They automatically execute an action (like sending money) when certain conditions are met.
- *Example:* "If the user sends 1 ETH, send them 100 XYZ Tokens."

---

## **4. Gas Fees**
Running code on the blockchain costs "Gas." This is a small fee paid to the people (Miners/Validators) who keep the network running.
